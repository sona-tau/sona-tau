    Trivial toki pona Substitutions

    Sona Tau Estrada Rivera
    Published on November 16, 2025, modified on December 6, 2025.

    toki open

    There is a family of programming languages that are called “Trivial Brainf*ck Substitutions” (TBFS for short). They basically take the 8 characters used in Brainf*ck and represent them using other strings of characters. This is (somewhat) the same idea behind sitelen pona and sitelen kuwi. I would even guess that it’s the same idea behind sitelen sitelen, but I don’t know enough yet about sitelen sitelen to make that conclusion.

    https://esolangs.org/wiki/Trivial_brainfuck_substitution

    FAQ

    • Why did you call it sitelen ike?
      • This script goes against the very nasin behind toki pona (IMO). So, it is “ike.”
    • Why did you do this?
      • I was recently made aware of sitelen kuwi and sitelen telo. Though I really disagree with the philosphy behind sitelen telo, I really like sitelen kuwi! This really reminded me of TBFS so I tried doing the same for toki pona and ended up with that piece of text up there.

    https://clockwise3411.github.io/lipu-sona-pi-sitelen-Kuwi/

    https://sona.pona.la/wiki/sitelen_telo

    I would like to see some TTPS made for other natural languages. To me, sitelen ike is somewhat readable.
