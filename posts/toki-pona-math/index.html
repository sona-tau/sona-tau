From my perception, toki pona does not have lots of common mathematical definitions.

However, they do have numbers some number systems that are well in use. For example, "nasin nanpa pona" which is used in texts like "su" to express the dates in which the story takes place. I must confess that I am mildly biased towards the seximal number system, but I digress, the "nasin nanpa pona" number system is equally as valid as the seximal number system.

To learn more about these, check out the following links:

  • toki pona number systems
  • nasin nanpa pona

From here on out, we will be using the "nasin nanpa pona" number system.
