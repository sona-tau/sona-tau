    Mathematics in toki pona

    Sona Tau Estrada Rivera
    Published on December 2, 2024, modified on December 6, 2025.

    nasin nanpa lon toki-pona

    The language toki pona (toki-pona) lacks a lot of common mathematical definitions. Although this is on purpose, I do enjoy talking about mathematics. In a language where kindness and compassion is so prevalent I see no reason why to avoid talking about something I feel so passionate about. Then, here is my documentation for how to speak about common mathematical terms.

    始めます

    In toki-pona we have the general notion of numbers as defined in “nasin nanpa pona” (nasin nanpa pona). I will perhaps go through the core ideas of this number system later. For now, keep in mind that it is a very simple number system. In this document I attempt to talk about complex mathematical concepts in toki-pona. That means that in order to read this, you must be aware of how nasin nanpa pona works. Again, it is a very simple system and I highly encourage you to read up on it if you haven’t.

    There is no need to reinvent the wheel, specially in mathematics. I will use the standard mathematical symbols to refer to the four basic operations:

    • (a + b) addition
    • (a - b) subtraction
    • (a \times b) multiplication
    • (\frac{a}{b}) division

    Other common ascii symbols that I refer to are:

    • Hindu-Arabic numeral system.
    • equality
    • exponentiation
    • parentheses

    The reason for this is that, personally, I consider mathematics to be its own language. Talking about Mathematics (the language) in toki-pona should be the same as talking about any other language in toki-pona. The language of Mathematics has its own culture and conventions and, depending on where on the planet you grew up in, your ideas about Mathematics can be different. Knowing this, I fully expect that different people will have different opinions on how Mathematics in toki-pona should be spoken.

    From here on out, I will be using the "nasin nanpa pona" number system.

    nasin nanpa ponaI would much rather use seximal for this. However, most people use nasin nanpa pona and since I want this to be digestable to the most amount of people, I shall use that instead.
